## 🌍 Working in Humanitarian Crisis Zones

**Current Context:** Cox's Bazar Refugee Settlement
- Population: 970,000+ Rohingya refugees
- Camp density: 47,000 people/km² (world's highest)
- Infrastructure: Severe deficits requiring offline-first technology
- Health access: Designed systems for 200+ daily OPD visits

**Key Challenges Addressed:**
- ⚠️ 60% of country inaccessible during monsoon season
- ⚠️ Intermittent internet (variable broadband coverage)
- ⚠️ Resource scarcity (limited devices, power constraints)
- ⚠️ Language barriers (Rohingya/Bangla/English fluency)

**Technology Principle:**
> *The distance between high-level policy (WHO guidelines) and working tools available to health workers is a structural failure that causes real-world harm. Humanitarian digital infrastructure should be "not just software"—it should be foundational technology for civilization.*
