## Health Programs

### 1. Health Outreach Program (HOP)
**Scope:** Community-based health services through 1,000+ Community Health Workers (CHWs)  
**Services:**
- Household health screening
- Fever/ARI/AWD case identification
- Pregnancy identification & ANC referrals
- Immunization (EPI) tracking
- MUAC screening (nutrition)
- Health education & hygiene promotion
- Scabies/skin disease screening
- Mass Drug Administration (MDA) coordination

**Coverage:** 3,524+ households surveyed (DHS 2026)  
**Reporting:** Daily household visit logs → Weekly compilation → Monthly master files

### 2. Health Post Program
**Facility UID:** HF-418 (WHO/Health Sector registered)  
**Location:** Camp 1W, Block B  
**GPS:** 21.21361055°N, 92.1507723°E  

**Services:**
- General outpatient care (200+ daily visits)
- Emergency medical stabilization
- Reproductive health services
- Mental health & psychosocial support (MHPSS)
- Diagnostic testing (RDT, basic labs)
- Pharmaceutical dispensing

**Recent Upgrades (Jan–May 2026):**
- Integrated Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) diagnostics
- Hired dedicated laboratory assistant
- Permanent metal fencing (replaced bamboo)
- Service Delivery SOPs finalized

### 3. Hepatitis C (HCV) Program
**Strategy:** WHO 90-90-90 cascade (Test → Diagnose → Treat → Cure)  
**Current Caseload:** 700+ patients in treatment care  
**Services:**
- Community screening (CHW-led)
- Rapid diagnostic testing (RDT)
- Treatment initiation (Direct-Acting Antivirals)
- Sustained Virological Response (SVR) monitoring
- Lost-to-follow-up tracking & recovery

**Key Indicators:**
- % of estimated HCV+ who are tested
- % of diagnosed linked to treatment
- % of treated achieving SVR (cured)
- Treatment enrollment rate
- Screening rate by camp/block

### 4. CVD/NCD Program
**Strategy:** WHO PEN (Package of Essential Noncommunicable Disease Interventions)  
**Conditions:** Hypertension, Diabetes, COPD, other NCDs  
**Current Caseload:** Estimated 500+ active patients  
**Services:**
- Screening (BP, blood glucose, risk stratification)
- Treatment initiation & medication provision
- Quarterly follow-up visits
- Medication refill management
- Complication tracking (stroke, MI, amputation, blindness)

**Key Indicators:**
- Screening coverage (%)
- Treatment enrollment rate
- BP control rate (<140/90)
- Diabetes control rate
- Medication adherence rate
- Lost-to-follow-up rate

### 5. Health System Strengthening (HSS)
**Partners:**
- 250-Bed District Sadar Hospital (Emergency Department support)
- RTMI (Research, Training & Management International) — Camp 5 & 17
- Friendship (NGO) — Camp 5 sentinel site

**Activities:**
- Clinical staff deployment (5 MBBS doctors to ED)
- Diagnostic equipment & reagent supply
- Infection Prevention & Control (IPC) support
- NCD corner establishment in partner clinics
- Training & SOP standardization

## Data Reporting Architecture

### **5-Tier Reporting Flow**

**Tier 1 (Community Level):**
- CHW daily household visit logs
- RDT screening results
- Paper forms → Weekly compilation → Digitization

**Tier 2 (Health Post Level):**
- OPD patient registry (daily entries)
- NCD patient follow-up log
- Pharmacy dispensing records
- Laboratory sample tracking

**Tier 3 (Program Management Level):**
- Master patient line lists
- Referral tracking
- HCV cascade dashboard
- M&E performance indicators
- Stored on Google Drive & shared sheets

**Tier 4 (Sector/Government Level):**
- DHIS2 submission (daily/monthly morbidity)
- EWARS weekly report (disease surveillance)
- 4W monthly activity report (sector coordination)
- HeRAMS facility assessment

**Tier 5 (Strategic Level):**
- Monthly progress reports (CPI HQ)
- Quarterly donor reports
- Annual service delivery results
- Strategic situation reports (SitReps)
