## 🏥 About My Projects

### What is ZarishSphere?

**ZarishSphere** is an open-source health information system (HIS) designed specifically for:
- Offline-first operation
- Resource-constrained environments
- Humanitarian and crisis settings
- Rohingya refugee health programs

**Status:** Currently in V1 development  
**Tech Stack:** Go, FHIR R5, PostgreSQL, Docker  
**License:** Apache 2.0 (code) + CC BY 4.0 (docs)

### What is ZARISH-INDEX?

**ZARISH-INDEX** is a comprehensive registry of 40+ health IT and humanitarian standards with 88K+ entries.

**Purpose:** Provide a single authoritative reference for:
- Health information standards
- Interoperability protocols
- Humanitarian tech specifications
- Compliance requirements

### What is ZARISH-STANDARDS?

**ZARISH-STANDARDS** is a classification framework for organizing digital health standards in humanitarian contexts.

**Purpose:** Create a hierarchy that helps:
- Developers choose appropriate standards
- Organizations align systems
- Projects ensure interoperability

### What is ZarishLog?

**ZarishLog** is a technical blueprint for humanitarian supply chain and inventory management systems.

**Covers:**
- Warehouse and inventory management
- Organizational asset tracking
- Gift-in-kind donation management
- Financial integration and auditing
- Performance reporting

## 🌍 About Humanitarian Work

### Why humanitarian tech?

Because **the distance between high-level policy (WHO guidelines) and the actual tools available to health workers is a structural failure that causes real-world harm.**

Humanitarian digital infrastructure should be:
- Not just software
- Foundational technology for civilization
- Accessible to the world's most vulnerable populations
- Designed for real constraints (offline, low-bandwidth, resource scarcity)

### What makes humanitarian tech different?

**Humanitarian context requires:**
- Offline-first design (assuming no internet)
- Extreme resource constraints (minimal devices/power)
- Multiple languages and scripts (Rohingya, Bangla, Arabic, etc.)
- Security in volatile environments
- Rapid deployment in crises
- Interoperability with government and partner systems

### How does ZarishSphere address this?

- ✅ Works completely offline
- ✅ Minimal hardware requirements
- ✅ Built-in language support
- ✅ Open-source for transparency
- ✅ Standards-based for interoperability
- ✅ No vendor lock-in
